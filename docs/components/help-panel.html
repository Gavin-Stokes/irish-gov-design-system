Help panel

A persistent footer panel that gives citizens access to support throughout the application journey.

Component WCAG 2.1 AA

Styling

  • Background: #004D44 (Deep Forest Green) — same as hero
  • Dividers between rows: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.2)
  • Text colour: #EAF4F5 (light teal) — achieves sufficient contrast on dark green
  • Icons: white, 20px
  • No outer shadow — the panel is full-width and butts against the screen edge

When to use

  • Include the help panel on every screen of the application flow.
  • Limit to two or three support options maximum — the panel should be concise, not comprehensive.
  • Always include the digital assistant as the first option to reduce phone call volume.

When not to use

  • Do not use the help panel as a general navigation footer — it is specifically for citizen support.
  • Do not include promotional content or non-support links in the panel.
  • check_circle Wrap the panel in <section aria-label="Help and support"> or <aside aria-label="Help and support"> to create a navigable landmark region. 1.3.6 / best practice
  • check_circle Phone numbers must use href="tel:+353..." so mobile devices can initiate a call directly. Display the number in human-readable format with international prefix. 2.1.1 A
  • check_circle External links (privacy policy) must include a visually hidden indicator and target="_blank" rel="noopener". Warn users they are leaving the service. 3.2.2 A
  • check_circle White text #FFFFFF and light teal #EAF4F5 on the dark green #004D44 background both achieve well above 4.5:1 contrast. Do not use any lighter or lower-contrast text colour. 1.4.3 AA
  • check_circle Each row in the panel must be an <a> element with a descriptive label. The row touch target must meet 44px minimum height. 2.5.5 / 4.1.2 A
  • check_circle Icons within the panel rows are decorative — they repeat the meaning of the adjacent text. Apply aria-hidden="true" to all panel icons. 1.1.1 A
