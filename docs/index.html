What is this?

This design system provides the visual language, components, and patterns used to build citizen-facing digital services for the Irish Government. It was developed for the Life Events discovery project — led by the Department of Public Expenditure and Reform (DPENDR), with design by Deloitte — and is centred on the Social Housing Application as the primary product in scope.

The system covers everything from colour tokens and typography to full interaction patterns, with accessibility built into every component from the ground up.

info
All public-facing components in this system are designed to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Accessibility notes are included on every component and pattern page.

Design principles

Citizen-first

Every design decision begins with the citizen's needs. Services speak directly to people in warm, plain English — second person throughout, no jargon, no bureaucratic framing. People dealing with major life events need clarity, not complexity.

Accessible by default

Accessibility is not a layer added at the end — it is a requirement embedded in every token, component, and pattern. The minimum target is WCAG 2.1 AA for all public-facing surfaces. This means correct colour contrast, visible focus states, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and meaningful touch targets on every interactive element.

Mobile-first

The Social Housing Application is designed for a 390px mobile canvas. Citizens will apply for housing on the phone they have — often in challenging circumstances. Every component is built for thumb-friendly use with one hand, in interrupted sessions, on varying network quality.

Consistent and trustworthy

The Irish Government Harp mark and the deep green brand palette signal to citizens that they are using a genuine government service. Consistent patterns across screens reduce cognitive load and build trust. Deviating from the system undermines both.

Plain English

Sentence case on all labels and CTAs. Action-oriented copy. Time estimates before multi-step flows. Complex government terms explained inline. No emoji. No title case. No passive voice. The Irish Government Tone of Voice guidelines apply throughout.

Products in scope

Social Housing Application (primary)

A mobile-first (390px) citizen-facing application for applying for social housing in a single, streamlined flow. Screens include: Home, Before You Begin, Personal Details, Employment, Income, Housing Requirements, Previous Housing, Success, Profile, and a 15-month review.

Service blueprints and journey maps

Internal DPENDR/Deloitte worksheets mapping as-is and target service journeys. These use separate visual conventions (swimlane diagrams, qualitative annotations) and are not covered by this component system.
